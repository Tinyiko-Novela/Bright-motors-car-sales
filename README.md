# Bright motors car sales

Bright Motors is a major automotive retailer in the United states which operates over 14000 dealerships continent-wide.
Recently, a new sales director has been appointed whose mission is to expand the dealership network, improve sales performance, and optimize inventory.
As the data analyst for Bright motors, I have been tasked with extracting insights which answer the below objectives from the historical data of Bright motors.

# Objectives of the case study
- Which car makes and models generate the most revenue
- The relationship between price, mileage, and year of manufacture
- Which regions or locations have the highest sales volumes
- Emerging trends in customer purchasing preferences
- Recommendations to increase dealership profitability and efficiency

# Findings

# Emerging trends in customer purchasing preferences
The highest number of purchases occur in the winter months.
Wednesday is the busiest day of the week and people prefer buying cars between 00:00 and 06:00.


# Which car makes and models generate the most revenue
Ford makes the highest revenue
Out of the 10 best models, 5 are from Ford. 
From the analysis, it is evident that consumers prefer budget friendly and reliable cars.

# The relationship between price, mileage, and year of manufacture
Customers prefer new/low mileage cars to older ones.
70% of customers are buying cars that cost less than $10000.
Very old models(21-44 years) cost less and have low sales

# Which regions or locations have the highest sales volumes
Florida has the highest revenue
10 states account for 75% of the revenue

# Recommendations to increase dealership profitability and efficiency
Reduce the number of dealerships in low performing states and expand on the best performing ones
Increase selling price to increase profit margins
Manage inventory levels and order based on need
Pay sales people by commission to encourage hard work
Ensure expenses stay under budget so that it doesn’t eat into profit
Launch sales and marketing campaigns for budget friendly cars
Consider reducing the work week from 7 days to three days to cut operational costs.
